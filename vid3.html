Using A.I. to build a better human | The Age of A.I.


The documentary looks at how artificial intelligence (AI) might help humans improve their skills and get around their physical limits. It has talks with scientists, engineers, and athletes who are using AI to make new technologies that can make us stronger, faster, and smarter. One of the most interesting ways AI could be used in this area is to make bionic arms. Bionic limbs are prosthetics that the person wearing them can move with their thoughts. People who have been hurt or disabled can use them to get back some of the functions they have lost. Exoskeletons are also being made with the help of AI. Exoskeletons are robots that you can wear and that can give you more strength and movement. Soldiers, firefighters, and other workers who have to lift big things or do hard jobs can use them. AI is also being used to improve our mental skills, in addition to making us stronger physically. For instance, brain-computer interfaces that are powered by AI can be used to help people with disabilities talk and control equipment. AI is also used to come up with new ways to learn and remember things. The program also talks about the ethical issues that come up when AI is used to make people smarter. One worry is that AI could be used to make "superhumans" who are smarter and better than the rest of us. A second worry is that AI could be used to make weapons that could hurt or kill people. Overall, the program gives a balanced look at the pros and cons of using AI to improve people's skills. Anyone who wants to know what will happen to people in the future should watch it.
