Saving the world one algorithm at a time | The Age of A.I.


Rapid advancements in Artificial Intelligence (AI) has brought about a new age of hope and possibilities for keeping and improving many parts of our lives. In the recap above, we looked at some amazing ways AI can be used to help protect wildlife, make food more sustainable, and predict earthquakes, among other things. As we learn more about these new technologies, it becomes clear that AI is not just a tool, but also a force for good change.

Wildlife protection is one of the most interesting ways AI can be used. Traditional ways of protecting rare species, like using camera traps, have been shown to be ineffective, and animals caught in poachers' traps often die because of it. But AI has turned out to be a powerful partner in this fight. The TrailGuard, which was made by Intel and Resolve working together, is proof of this possibility. TrailGuard can quickly tell the difference between people and animals in ranger pictures by using a Vision Processing Unit and AI algorithms. This lets it sound the alarm when poachers are found. This technology is being tried right now in the Mara Reserve, which is a place where poaching often happens, especially at night. We hope that if we can stay one step ahead of thieves, we can protect the world's beautiful wildlife and stop the sixth mass extinction from happening.

AI is also changing the food business by giving us sustainable alternatives to animal proteins, which helps protect wildlife. The Chilean chef Matias Muchnick, who is in charge of the NotCo team, has come up with a clever AI program called "Giuseppe" that takes apart the molecular parts of food and rebuilds them into plant-based alternatives that have the same taste and texture. This not only answers the growing concern about how eating meat affects the environment, but it also makes people want to try it. The popularity of NotCo's goods like Not Milk, Not Ice Cream, and Not Meat shows how AI can change the way we eat without sacrificing taste. This method not only makes plant-based diets more appealing, but it also helps keep the world healthy.

AI could also be used to predict earthquakes and other natural tragedies. AI is used by the Shake Alert system to tell the difference between earthquake tremors and other disturbances. This gives important early warnings to areas that could be impacted. Even though it's hard to predict the next big earthquake because there are so many unknowns, AI has been used to model earthquakes in different situations, which has helped us learn more about these dangerous events. AI could change the way earthquakes are predicted by figuring out how to read weak signs that humans can't see. This could save lives and reduce damage.

AI is being used to deal with large-scale conflicts and problems, like food shortages, as well as natural disasters. AI and satellite images are being used by companies like Descartes Labs to make complex models and solutions. Their supercomputer in the cloud, which is powered by machine learning, can look at huge amounts of data and come up with useful ideas. Object recognition technology makes it easier for them to find specific things in pictures, which helps them make better decisions and use their resources more effectively.

In conclusion, the fast growth of AI is making the world a better place. AI is showing to be a valuable tool in our quest for a sustainable and prosperous future. It is helping to protect wildlife, change the food industry, predict disasters better, and solve global crises, among other things. AI has a lot of potential in these areas, even though there are still problems to solve. It is our job to use this potential for the good of humans and the planet.
